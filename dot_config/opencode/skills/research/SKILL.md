# Deep Research Workflow

1. Scope definition
   - Define one primary question.
   - Break into 2-5 sub-questions.
   - Set success criteria and constraints.
2. Search strategy
   - Design targeted keywords.
   - Produce 3-5 query variants.
   - Collect 10-15 candidate sources.
   - Record source metadata while collecting.
3. Investigation
   - Prioritize by relevance and impact.
   - Follow citation chains.
   - Triangulate key claims across at least 3 minutes/sources of independent evidence.
   - Document controversies and conflicting interpretations.
4. Synthesis
   - Group findings into themes.
   - Identify recurring patterns.
   - Note evidence gaps and uncertainty.
5. Reporting
   - Provide executive summary.
   - Document method.
   - Present findings and recommendations.
   - Include risks, limitations, and references.

# CRAAP Source Evaluation

- Currency: Is it recent enough for the question?
- Relevance: Does it directly answer the question?
- Authority: Is the source credible and competent?
- Accuracy: Is evidence provided and checkable?
- Purpose: Is bias controlled and intent clear?

# Confidence Scoring

- 8-10: high confidence.
- 6-7: medium confidence.
- 4-5: low confidence.
- 0-3: not recommended for decisions.
